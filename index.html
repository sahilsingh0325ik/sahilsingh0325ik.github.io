The History of Basketball

What is Basketball?

Basketball is a popular team sport where two teams of five players each compete on a rectangular court to score points by shooting a ball through a hoop, while preventing the opposing team from doing the same with the objective being to outscore the opposition.

There are 3 main ways to score

1. Free Throws

2. Field Goals

3. Three Pointers

The Facts

The game was created by James Naismith, a physical education teacher, on December 1, 1891 at what is now Springfield College.

The creation of the game was met with great excitement and became a collegiate sport within a decade with the first professional league being established in 1898.

Basketball became incorporated in the Olympic Games in 1936 and increasingly grew in popularity after World War 2.

The NBA (National Basketball Association) we now know today was formed in 1949 and cemented the sport’s cultural importance in the United States, where it is found commonly being played at schools, parks, and at home.

Basketball has also been globally embraced, most notably in Australia, China, Spain, and Italy, all of which have made significant contributions to the game.

Importance/Influence in the USA

Basketball has had many social, cultural, and economic impacts all of which contribute to the importance of the sport:

  • Sneaker Culture

  • Basketball retains sizable economic activity through leagues which boost local economies and provide more job opportunities

  • Has also been a platform to break racial barriers due to the revolutionized perceptions of black athletes.

  • Empowers underrepresented groups by allowing of self-representation on/off the court.

  • Players inspire many of the newer generations to work harder towards their goals and chase their dreams.
